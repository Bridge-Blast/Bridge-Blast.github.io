What is Bridge Blast?

Bridge Blast is an app-first guide to the official Blast bridge for moving assets between Ethereum and Blast Mainnet. Blast is an EVM-compatible optimistic rollup with native yield for ETH and stablecoins, as described in the Blast docs.

How Bridge Blast works

  1. Open the official routeUse blast.io/en/bridge and confirm the blast.io domain before connecting a wallet.
  2. Connect walletChoose the wallet that holds ETH on Ethereum or the account you use on Blast Mainnet.
  3. Choose directionUse Deposit to move ETH to Blast Mainnet or Withdraw to send funds back to Ethereum.
  4. Review and signCheck token, network, amount, and wallet prompts before approving any transaction.

Before you bridge

Official bridge focus

The official Blast page includes Deposit, Withdraw, and History views, with wallet connection required before signing.

Network clarity

Blast Mainnet uses ETH as its currency symbol and chain ID 81457, listed in the network information docs.

Post-bridge checks

After bridging, use wallet activity or Blastscan to review transaction status without relying on screenshots or third-party claims.

Bridge Blast FAQ

What is bridge blast?

Bridge Blast is a destination page that routes users to the official Blast bridge at blast.io/en/bridge; it is not a wallet or custodial service.

Is Bridge Blast the official Blast bridge?

No. The official action happens on blast.io, so verify the domain and each wallet prompt before signing.

Can I use bridge blast to move ETH to Blast Mainnet?

Use this page as a shortcut, then complete the deposit on the official bridge; Blast Mainnet uses ETH as its currency symbol and chain ID 81457.

Does Bridge Blast set bridge fees or rates?

No. Gas, route availability, and wallet prompts come from the official app, wallet, and underlying networks; review them before signing.

How do I check a Bridge Blast transaction?

After using the official bridge, check your wallet activity or search the transaction on Blastscan.
